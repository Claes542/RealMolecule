I am pleased to submit for your consideration the manuscript

**"A Coulomb Route to Solar Fusion: p + e + p → d and d + d → ⁴He"**

for publication in *Progress in Physics* as a research article. In accordance with the journal's
guidelines the manuscript is submitted as a preliminary PDF; a LaTeX version prepared in the
`pip-template` will be provided upon acceptance.

**What the paper does.** It places two accounts of solar hydrogen burning side by side. In the standard
proton–proton chain the first, rate-limiting step is the *weak* reaction p+p → d + e⁺ + ν, which
converts a proton into a neutron and emits a neutrino. In the RealNucleus picture — where the neutron is
a bound proton–electron pair and the deuteron is 2p+1e — the same step is purely *electromagnetic*:
p + e + p → d, a plasma electron captured as the deuteron's glue, with no flavour change, no positron,
and no neutrino. The chain closes with the directly-computed fusion d + d → ⁴He. Both routes net to
4p + 2e⁻ → ⁴He at 26.73 MeV, differing only in the ~2% the standard chain gives to neutrinos.

**The central argument, and its honest limits.** The clean distinction is one of lepton bookkeeping: in
the Coulomb route no lepton is created or destroyed — the electron persists as the deuteron's
constituent — so lepton-number conservation requires no neutrino, whereas the standard neutrino is the
signature of the flavour-changing weak vertex p→n. The paper is explicit about what this does and does
not establish. It examines the original "desperate" need for the neutrino (continuous beta spectrum and
spin–statistics) and shows the energy–momentum half lapses for a *capture* (p+e+p → d+γ emits the
definite 2.22 MeV deuteron line), while the spin–statistics half does not, and is flagged as the open
problem of the electron-in-nucleus picture. It concedes the neutrino is indispensable to the Standard
Model and rests on evidence broader than 1930, while noting that this evidence is theory-laden and that
the neutrino's epistemic standing is nonetheless weaker than that of the electron and proton. The claim
is deliberately bounded: not that the neutrino is dispensable across physics, but that the entry step of
solar burning may be electromagnetic rather than weak.

**Why it is falsifiable.** The two accounts are sharply opposed at one observable — the solar neutrino
flux, locked to the luminosity at ~2 ν per ⁴He and directly measured (Borexino, SNO). The paper states
this as the experimentum crucis rather than minimising it. Every computation is reproducible via an
open-source WebGPU RealQM solver (https://claes542.github.io/RealMolecule/gallery.html; source
https://github.com/Claes542/RealMolecule).

**Declarations.** The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has not been
previously published. There are no competing interests. The physical argument and model are my own; the
numerical implementation was developed and run with AI assistance (Claude, Anthropic). A companion
paper, *RealNucleus: Nuclear Binding as Dual Coulomb Confinement*, provides the nuclear computations on
which this paper draws and is cited accordingly.
