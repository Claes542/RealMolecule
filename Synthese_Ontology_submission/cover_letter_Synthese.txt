To the Editors-in-Chief, Synthese

Dear Editors,

I submit for your consideration the manuscript "Charge Densities in Real Space: RealQM and the
Realist Quest Carried by Schroedinger" for publication in Synthese.

The paper takes up a question in the history and philosophy of quantum mechanics -- whether the
microscopic world is of the same form as the macroscopic, extended matter in three-dimensional space
only of smaller size, or something categorically other -- and connects it to two current debates that
are usually treated separately:

  (i) the arena of the wave function: wave-function realism (Albert, Ney) versus the primitive-ontology
      programme (Maudlin, Allori, Duerr-Goldstein-Zanghi, Esfeld), with spacetime state realism
      (Wallace & Timpson) as a middle route; and
  (ii) the individuality of identical particles: whether permutation-invariant quantum particles are
      individuals and whether the Principle of the Identity of Indiscernibles survives (French & Krause;
      Saunders; Muller & Saunders; Muller & Seevinck; Caulton & Butterfield).

My central observation is that both problems are consequences of two features of one and the same
formula -- the antisymmetric N-particle amplitude on 3N-dimensional configuration space: the
high-dimensional domain generates the arena problem, and the antisymmetry generates the individuality
problem. I then examine a real-space charge-density formulation ("RealQM"), which revives Schroedinger's
original 1926 reading of the wave function as a charge density in ordinary space -- the anschaulich
programme displaced by the Bohr-Born-Heisenberg-Dirac turn precisely because, for many electrons, the
amplitude moves onto 3N-dimensional configuration space. RealQM keeps N unit charge densities on
non-overlapping regions of R^3, and so has neither of the two features: it is a primitive ontology in
three-space, and it individuates its electrons absolutely, by the regions they occupy, so that the
Identity of Indiscernibles is satisfied. The two virtues are a single geometric fact -- the non-overlap
of the regions does double duty -- and Pauli exclusion and the identity of indiscernibles coincide.

I give the argument in explicit formulas, on the view that here philosophy is made more precise with
them than without. A substantial section argues that the decisive asset is computational: the
3N-dimensional amplitude is intractable beyond very small N -- practice already retreats to effective
three-dimensional objects, and density-functional theory makes the 3D density fundamental in practice --
whereas RealQM computes directly with its beables in R^3, and the same energy functional has been used
to compute atoms across the periodic table, molecular bonds and hydrogen bonds, and, reading the nucleus
as protons and electrons bound by Coulomb alone, nuclear binding energies. That one parameter-poor
functional reproduces observation across five orders of magnitude in scale is offered, on no-miracles
grounds, as evidence that the model tracks real structure. A section places the proposal against the
point-particle ontology of the Standard Model; a further section meets the main objections; and a
closing reflection reads the result as a Wittgensteinian dissolution of the two problems, arguing that
this is not deflationary but a redirection toward the nature of physical reality itself. I am careful
throughout to bound the claim: RealQM is a rival theory whose warrant is its computed chemistry and
nuclear structure, and the metaphysical verdict is explicitly conditional on that empirical adequacy.

I believe the paper is a good fit for Synthese's philosophy-of-physics readership: it engages the
current wave-function-realism / primitive-ontology and identity-and-individuality literatures directly,
sets them in their historical frame, and offers a single, formally precise move that bears on both.

The manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and has a single author. In keeping
with Synthese's double-anonymous review policy, I enclose a blinded manuscript with all identifying
information removed (including several self-referential companion works, whose details are withheld in
the bibliography), together with a separate title page carrying the author information, declarations,
and the withheld reference details.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
