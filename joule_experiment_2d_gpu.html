Joule's experiment in 2D, compressible Navier–Stokes, WebGPU. Two chambers connected by a channel opening at the top. Left chamber has ten times the density of the right; both start at the same temperature (e/ρ = 1). The gas expands through the channel; the density relaxes toward the mean and the temperature stays close to uniform.

Display: blue tracer particles advected by the velocity field v = m/ρ, with fading trails and periodic reset (the original p5 sketch's display). Wall column in grey, channel opening at the bottom. Two line cuts overlay the profiles of density (cyan), pressure p=γe (red) and temperature T=e/ρ (green): one at mid height, one across the channel.

Grid: 100² = 10 000 cells. State packed as vec4<f32>(ρ, e, mₓ, m_y) per cell. Walls use free-slip ghost cells (reflected normal momentum, tangential kept) at the fluid faces.
