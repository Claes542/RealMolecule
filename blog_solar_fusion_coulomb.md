# Solar Fusion by Coulomb Alone

**Claim in one line:** the Sun may shine by the *electric* force alone — no strong force, no weak force, and no relativity required.

## The standard story

Textbook solar physics has the Sun burn hydrogen to helium through the proton–proton chain, whose first step is

$$p + p \;\to\; d + e^{+} + \nu_e.$$

This is a **weak-force** reaction: one of the two protons is turned into a neutron, emitting a positron and a neutrino. It is famously slow — the weak force acting only in the fleeting instant two protons touch is what makes the Sun's fuel last ten billion years — and it sends a flood of neutrinos streaming out of the Sun.

## The Coulomb story

In the RealNucleus picture, a nucleus is nothing but **protons and electrons as charge clouds**, held together by the ordinary **Coulomb** attraction — the same force that binds atoms and molecules, read with the charges rearranged. The neutron is not elementary; it is a bound proton–electron pair. The deuteron is therefore **2 protons + 1 electron**: two positive charges glued by one negative one, the nuclear cousin of the molecular ion H₂⁺.

Then the first step of hydrogen burning is not weak but **purely electromagnetic**:

$$p + e + p \;\to\; d.$$

An electron — always abundant in the solar plasma — is simply captured as the deuteron's glue. **No proton is transmuted, no positron is created, and no neutrino is required.** Two deuterons then fuse to helium, and the whole chain

$$4\,\mathrm{H} \;=\; 4p + 4e \;\to\; {}^4\mathrm{He} + 2e$$

releases the same **26.7 MeV** as the standard account — differing only in the ~2% the standard chain gives up to neutrinos.

## What supports it

- **Binding energies come out right.** Built from one repeating unit — a (2 electrons + 4 protons) alpha — the light nuclei reproduce their measured binding to ~107%, from Coulomb forces and a single scale fixed on the deuteron, with nothing else fitted.
- **The electron's mass turns out to be irrelevant.** In direct computation, the deuteron's electron is a *flat* charge cloud that fills the cage the two protons make; a genuinely light electron, relaxing on its own, chooses to be flat — so its mass drops out of the result entirely. The nuclear scale is set by the *heavy proton*, the atomic scale by the *light electron*: **two sizes, one Coulomb law.**
- **It is falsifiable.** The one sharp difference from the standard picture is the neutrino. The standard chain predicts a solar neutrino flux locked to the sunlight; the Coulomb picture predicts none from the first step. Solar neutrinos *are* measured — so the flux is the honest experimentum crucis, and the claim is stated to be tested by it, not around it.

## The real question — what would it mean for theoretical physics?

Suppose, for the sake of the argument, that this holds up. Then some load-bearing beams of modern physics would have to be looked at again:

- **Does stellar burning need the weak force at all?** If deuteron formation is an electromagnetic *capture* rather than a weak *conversion*, the weak interaction loses its starring role in how stars shine.
- **Does nuclear binding need the strong force?** If the same Coulomb law that binds atoms also binds nuclei — with the electron doing the gluing, sign-reversed — then the strong force is not the only candidate for what holds nuclei together.
- **Is mass an electromagnetic quantity at all?** If the electron's mass is irrelevant to nuclear structure, and *size* is the operative variable, then mass looks less like a player in the electric and nuclear game and more like the label of a scale — something that belongs, with gravitation, to a different sector.
- **How much of the Standard Model's structure is necessary, and how much is convention?** The neutrino was born in 1930 as a "desperate remedy" to balance the books of beta decay. If those books can be balanced electromagnetically in fusion, which of our particles and forces are *derived* and which are *assumed*?

None of this is settled — there are real open problems (the spin–statistics of the electron-in-nucleus, the closed-shell structure, and above all the measured neutrino flux). But the picture is simple, parameter-free, and computable, and it is offered exactly so that it can be checked.

**The question it leaves on the table is worth asking out loud: if the Sun can be made to shine by Coulomb alone, how much of the rest of theoretical physics is actually needed — and how much have we been assuming?**
