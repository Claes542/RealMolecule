Reading it. Cold, unmelted: is q high (~0.7–0.9) and coordination ≈4? → the isotropic Z=2 water holds the imposed tetrahedron. On heating: q falls, and if a 5th neighbour pulls in (d5 → ~3.2, coordination → ~5) that's the collapse to HDL. Two well-separated states along the melt = the two structures, captured in one run.
