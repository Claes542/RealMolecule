To the Editor
Physics Essays

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit, for consideration in Physics Essays, the single-author manuscript
"RealNucleus: Nuclear Binding as Dual Coulomb Confinement, without a Strong Force."

The paper asks a question the standard textbooks answer in the negative: can the atomic nucleus
be bound by Coulomb forces alone, with no strong force? Since Chadwick's neutron in 1932 it has
been a fixed point of physics that it cannot -- the protons all repel, and a short-range strong
(today, residual QCD) force is held to be necessary to hold the nucleus together. The manuscript
reopens that question by returning to the older proton-electron picture of the nucleus, but with
protons and electrons represented as equal-mass charge clouds on non-overlapping domains in the
manner of RealQM, interacting through Coulomb forces only.

The binding mechanism is what I call dual confinement: the negative electron clouds hold the
positive protons together against their mutual repulsion, while the positive protons cage the
electrons against theirs -- each species confining the other by the same Coulomb attraction read
from two sides. Calibrating a single energy scale on the deuteron (2.22 MeV), the alpha particle
comes out at 103% of its experimental binding with no fitting; the ladder 2H -> 4He -> 8Be -> 16O
binds as connected units with a nearly constant binding per nucleon (saturation), and the alpha
binding is precisely the energy released in hydrogen-to-helium fusion.

I am fully aware that this is an extraordinary claim, and the manuscript presents it as such --
not as a refutation of the strong force, but as a falsifiable demonstration that the possibility
of a purely Coulomb nucleus is computationally open. The classical objections that retired the
proton-electron model (the confinement-energy estimate, the nuclear magnetic moments) are stated
plainly as open, and the limitations of the present results (configurations selected rather than
yet derived; an effective length scale) are not concealed. Every result is reproducible in a web
browser, with the complete source code and the interactive gallery linked from the paper; for a
claim of this kind the runnable computation is the primary evidence, and I would encourage
referees to re-run the configurations for themselves.

I believe Physics Essays is the appropriate venue precisely because the journal engages serious,
carefully argued unconventional work that the mainstream nuclear journals decline on premise. The
manuscript is original, has not been published, and is not under consideration elsewhere; there
are no conflicts of interest. The work builds on RealQM (the foundational formulation, submitted
separately to Annales de la Fondation Louis de Broglie) but stands on its own.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.

Sincerely,
Claes Johnson
