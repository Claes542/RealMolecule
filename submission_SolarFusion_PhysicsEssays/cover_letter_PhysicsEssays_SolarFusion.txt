To the Editor
Physics Essays

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit, for consideration in Physics Essays, the single-author manuscript
"Solar Fusion by Coulomb Alone."

The essay argues that solar hydrogen burning can be carried through entirely by the electrostatic
attraction between protons and electrons -- with no strong force, no weak force, and, in the
relativity-free formulation adopted here, no appeal even to E=mc^2. In the standard proton-proton
chain the burning opens with p + p -> d + e+ + nu_e, a weak-interaction reaction that converts a
proton into a neutron, emits a positron and a neutrino, and is the slow, rate-limiting step that
makes the Sun's hydrogen last billions of years. In the RealNucleus picture, where the neutron is
a bound proton-electron pair and the deuteron is accordingly 2p+1e, the same first step is instead
purely electromagnetic: p + e + p -> d, an electron drawn from the plasma and captured as the
deuteron's glue, with no flavour change, no positron, and no neutrino. Two deuterons then fuse to
helium, d + d -> 4He, a reaction computed directly in RealQM. Both accounts net to the same
transmutation, 4p + 2e -> 4He at 26.73 MeV; they differ in mechanism and in lepton content.

I am deliberately careful about scope, and I would ask the referees to note this. The essay makes
no prediction for the solar neutrino flux and does not assert that neutrinos do not exist. Solar
neutrinos are observed, and where they arise is outside the model's scope. What is offered is
narrower and self-contained: a Coulomb mechanism for the burning that is well-defined, computable,
energetically exact for 4p + 2e -> 4He, and that as a property of the mechanism requires no
neutrino. The lepton bookkeeping is the cleanest distinction between the two routes -- in the
standard first step a proton is transmuted, forcing the creation of a positron and a neutrino,
whereas in the Coulomb route no lepton is created or destroyed. The essay also isolates where the
energy is actually generated (deuteron formation is only ~11% of the release; the fusion d + d ->
4He is ~89%, essentially the alpha-binding energy), and treats honestly the abundance question,
the rate/Coulomb-barrier comparison, and the one genuinely open problem of the electron-in-nucleus
picture (the spin-statistics bookkeeping of the "nitrogen anomaly").

Throughout, energies are taken as directly measured -- capture gammas, reaction Q-values,
photodisintegration thresholds -- rather than inferred from mass deficits, so the energetics owe
nothing to any mass-energy relation; a directly-measured energy release is, if anything, the more
model-free benchmark.

This manuscript is a companion to my "RealNucleus: Nuclear Binding as Dual Coulomb Confinement,"
currently under consideration at Physics Essays, on which it draws for the deuteron, alpha, and
d + d -> 4He computations but from which it stands independently. The manuscript is original, has
not been published, and is not under consideration elsewhere; there are no conflicts of interest.
In keeping with the journal's disclosure practice, I note that the numerical computations were
implemented with AI assistance (Claude, Anthropic); the physical argument, the claims, and the
text are my own.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.
