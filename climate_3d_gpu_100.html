Basic 3D climate model — Rayleigh-Bénard convection. Compressible Navier–Stokes in a closed cubic box, with a constant gravitational body force −g·ρ in the −y direction. The bottom wall (j = 0) is held at hot temperature T_hot; the top wall (j = N−1) at cold T_cold. The four side walls are closed (no-slip, Neumann ρ, e). When the bottom-to-top temperature contrast exceeds a critical Rayleigh number, the fluid develops convection cells: hot fluid rises in plumes, cools at the ceiling, sinks back down, transporting heat upward — the simplest model of atmospheric circulation.

Display: mid-z slice (xy plane through the centre of the box). Colour = density (blue low → white mid → red high), tuned to ρ ∈ [0.5, 1.5]. Black dots = forward-streak particle traces showing the instantaneous velocity field. Side panel: yz wake slice at i = 0.75·N.

Equations: ∂ρ/∂t + ∇·(ρv) = 0; ∂(ρv)/∂t + ∇·(ρv⊗v) + ∇p = ∇·σ − g·ρ·ŷ; ∂e/∂t + ∇·(e v) + p∇·v = ν|∇v|² + diffusion; p = γe, γ = 0.2.

Grid: 100³ = 1 000 000 cells. State packed as 8 floats per cell; ping-pong storage buffers of 32 MB each — runs on any WebGPU GPU.
