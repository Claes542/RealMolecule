Maybe RealQM Can Do What AlphaFold2 Cannot

AlphaFold2 cannot:
• Predict protein–protein docking from physics (it uses learned patterns, not forces)
• Handle drug–protein binding (no concept of non-protein molecules)
• Model chemical reactions (bond breaking/forming)
• Simulate phase changes (ice melting, water dynamics)
• Account for solvent effects (water driving folding and binding)
• Work with non-standard chemistry (metals, modified residues, novel molecules)

RealQM evidence so far:
• Protein–protein docking: coiled-coil helices dock from 12→7.5 Å, insulin A20–B19 closes from 12→7.5 Å — both blind, water-mediated, no inter-chain biases (coiled-coil, insulin)
• H-bond physics: formamide dimer H···O=2.04 Å, water dimer O–O=2.95 Å — matching experiment from electron density alone (formamide, water dimer)
• Nucleic acids: G:C base pair H-bonds at 2.1 Å, RNA hairpin stem self-assembly with G1:C12=1.99 Å (G:C pair, RNA hairpin)
• Bond breaking: H + H&sub2; → H&sub2; + H exchange through H&sub3; transition state (reaction)
• Phase change: ice crystal with correct O–O=2.73 Å, melting dynamics (ice, melting)
• Metallic bonding: Li lattice restores from perturbation (Li metal)
• Drug binding: acetaminophen + protein pocket — in progress (drug binding)

Open challenges: lock-and-key binding (concave grooves), long-range force accuracy (Poisson convergence), and proton transfer stability under long dynamics.
