The Fine-Structure Constant as a Size Ratio

H− and the deuteron as mirror proton–electron models — α² = Rp/a0. Part of the RealQM / RealNucleus program.
Status — a structural/interpretive proposal. The claim is the relation α² = Rp/a0 read off the mirror pair H− / deuteron; the underlying primitive (proton size, or the classical radius) is stated as open (§6). A cosmological extension is a brief outlook (§7) — see the companion Real Cosmology essay.

Abstract

RealQM and RealNucleus model atoms and nuclei from the same proton–electron Coulomb mechanics — charge densities minimising the Coulomb energy, no strong force, no exchange–correlation functional. Their simplest mirror pair is the subject here: the H− ion (one proton surrounded by two electrons in two shells) as the atom model, and the deuteron ²H (one electron caged inside two protons in two shells) as the nucleus model — the two related by p↔e. Both models take only three inputs: the charge ±e, an electron kinetic coefficient κ, and the proton size Rp — and no mass m, no speed of light c, no Planck constant ℏ (these never appear separately). The atomic size is a0 = 2κ/e² (electrons taper in vacuum, kinetic-set); the nuclear size is Rp (caged electron flat, zero kinetic energy, size-set). Their ratio is α² = Rp/a0 (a0 = size of the H atom), so α = √(Rp/a0) is a pure length ratio, independent of m, c, ℏ. Validated: atomic total energies <1% across the periodic table, and the He-4/deuteron binding ratio 13.1 vs 12.7.

1. Two Coulomb models: RealQM and RealNucleus

The program treats protons and electrons as ± unit charges (charge densities) interacting by the Coulomb law, with a kinetic penalty on the electron densities; structure is found by minimising the total energy.

  • RealQM — atoms and molecules: electron charge clouds in non-overlapping shells around small proton nuclei, bound by attraction to the nuclear charge. No basis set, no exchange–correlation functional. Reproduces atomic total energies to <1% across the periodic table.
  • RealNucleus — nuclei: protons bound by shared electrons (neutron = p+e), by the same Coulomb attraction, with no strong force. Nested charge-cloud shells give the He-4/deuteron binding-energy ratio 13.1 vs 12.7 experimental.

Both are the same mechanics — charges and a kinetic coefficient — run at two scales, locked together by one geometric relation, α² = Rp/a0.

2. The mirror pair: H− and the deuteron

Take equal numbers of protons and electrons as ± charges on the same footing (motivated cosmologically in the companion). The simplest three-body bound systems are exact mirrors under p↔e:

They are the same 3-body Coulomb system — a central charge holding two like-charged partners against their mutual repulsion — differing in nothing but which particle is central. The surrounders set the size: electrons (fluid) ⇒ atomic; protons (rigid, fixed size Rp) ⇒ nuclear.

The inputs of both models. Charge ±e; an electron kinetic coefficient κ (taken as a single primitive, not decomposed as ℏ²/2m); and the proton size Rp. No mass, no c, no ℏ enters separately — only the combination κ, the charge e, and the length Rp. The construction is non-relativistic and electrostatic.

3. The two sizes, and α² = Rp/a0

An electron density ψ (∫ψ² = 1) of size L has energy E = κ∫|∇ψ|² − Z e²/L.

Atomic size (charge-set). Around a proton, an electron must taper to zero in vacuum; the varying profile costs kinetic energy ~κ/L², and minimising κ/L² − e²/L gives a0 = 2κ/e² (= ℏ²/me²), the size of the H atom.

Nuclear size (size-set). Caged between protons on its own domain with a multiphase free boundary, the electron's density does not drop to zero but hands off to the proton density; a flat caged density has ∇ψ = 0, hence zero kinetic energy. With no kinetic pressure to spread it, its size is set by the proton's finite size Rp (the cage floor), binding Coulomb at that size.

α² = Rp/a0 = Rpe²/2κ,    α = √(Rp/a0)

Written this way α is a ratio of two lengths, obtained by measuring two sizes and dividing: it carries no m, no c, no ℏ. This is the textbook re/a0 = α² (classical radius over Bohr radius) with Rp identified as re; the standard form e²/ℏc writes the same number through c (and m, ℏ), but the ratio needs none of them. In standard QM/QED α is the relativistic coupling (amplitude √α per photon vertex; fine-structure splitting ∝ α²; v1/c = α); here it is the mass-, c- and ℏ-free proton-to-atom size ratio — the more primitive reading.

4. Computations

Atomic scale. With the electron κ and charge e, a0 = 2κ/e² = 0.529 Å = 52,918 fm, and RealQM reproduces the H atom at this size with total energy −0.500 Ha (exact).

Nuclear scale and the ratio. Taking Rp at candidate nuclear lengths (true α² = 5.33×10−5, α = 1/137.04):

So with Rp at the classical-radius scale the relation is exact by construction; at the measured deuteron radius it reproduces the fine-structure constant to ~25% (α = 1/157 vs 1/137) — from charge, κ and the proton size alone, no strong force and no c. (The deuteron is loosely bound and slightly larger than a nucleon, so this is the scale/ratio being right, not a precise fit.)

Independent validation of the two models. Each side is quantitative:

  • Atom side: RealQM atomic total energies <1% across the whole periodic table (all Z ≥ 11; period-2 p-block B–F the only 3–6% exception) [atoms paper].
  • Nucleus side: RealNucleus He-4/deuteron binding ratio 13.1 vs 12.7 experimental (~3%, nested charge-cloud shells) [RealNucleus].

So α² = Rp/a0 is bracketed by two independent validations — atomic scale <1%, nuclear binding ratio ~3% — beyond the ~25% size check itself.

5. Mass is a separate quantity

Nothing above used mass: H− and the deuteron are built from charge and κ. Mass is inertia — the localisation (frequency / inverse size) of the matter wave — a separate, gravitational quantity, coupling to the electrostatic sector only through energy (E = mc²). Electromagnetism (charge) is an interaction; mass is the inverse size of each matter wave.

6. Complexity from the size asymmetry, and open problems

The proton–electron asymmetry that builds structure is not of charge (exactly balanced, ±e) but of size: the proton fixed-and-small (a rigid anchor), the electron variable. The fixed proton anchors; the variable electron spans scales — shells in the atom, a cage-filling cloud in the deuteron — the nested hierarchy that is complexity. α measures this asymmetry (inverse dynamic range 1/α² ≈ 18,800): the smallness of α is the engine of complexity.

Open: (i) the nuclear primitive — Rp as input length, or the classical radius re = α²a0 (electromagnetic mass); α's value is reinterpreted, not derived. (ii) Whether the solver realises a flat zero-KE caged electron at the proton scale is not cleanly confirmed. (iii) The equal-footing treatment of p and e vs. the measured mass ratio.

7. Speculative outlook

The same ingredients extend, far more speculatively, to a cosmology — equal-mass ± charges from two fluctuating potentials, a gravitational bounce with negative-mass dark energy, and a primordial helium fraction Y ≈ 0.245 — in the companion Real Cosmology essay; noted here only for scope.

In one line. H− (1 proton, 2 electrons) and the deuteron (1 electron, 2 protons) are mirror proton–electron Coulomb systems; from three inputs (±e, κ, Rp; no m, c, ℏ) the atomic and nuclear sizes come out as a0 and Rp, and the fine-structure constant is their ratio, α² = Rp/a0.
